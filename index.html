Sagrada Familia

Must See

Sagrada Familia is one of these must see things once you're in Barcelona. It is touristy, but worth seeing from outside as well as inside at least once in a lifetime. Make sure to get tickets in advance as the line around the cathedral is always massive

Park Güell

Must See

The Park Güell is a public park system composed of gardens and architectonic elements located on Carmel Hill. UNESCO declared the park a World Heritage Site and it is another Barcelona must-see sight. It takes a little longer to get ther from the nearest underground stop. The park itself has no admission and is open to public, but there’s a part with the colorful mosaics that you’ll need to get a ticket for. You can even get it on the same day as when you’ll be going. But only certain number of people are permitted there at the time, so you’ll be given a time slot.

Address: 08024 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Gràcia (north)

Tickets available here


Barcelona Cathedral & The Old Town

Old Town

Barcelona Cathedral also known as the Cathedral of the Holy Cross and Saint Eulalia, is a famous example of fifteenth-century Catalan Gothic architecture. Definitely worth seeing, as well as Barri Gotic which is the city district of the town where the original cathedral is. It is only a short walk from famous la Rambla.

Address: Pla de la Seu, s/n, 08002 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Barri Gòtic


Plaza Espanya & Magic Fountain

Plaza Espanya

Magic Fountain is a “dancing” fountain on Plaza Espana. The shows are on between the Wed – Friday from 21:30 to 22:30. By the show they mean that the fountain is coordinated with the light and music. Then on Plaza Espanya you also have National Art museum of Catalunya and Arenas. Arenas are old bullfighting ring. When bull fights were forbidden in Catalunya this one was converted in the shopping mall. Here you can go on the roof and there’s a nice view from there.

Address: 08004 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Sants-Montjuïc


Passeig de Gràcia & La Rambla

If you're staying in L'Eixample, you’ll be probably staying close to Passeig de Gracia. It is just a long street on which you can find a few Gaudi’s buildings (which are quite photo worthy).

When you’ll be walking down the street from the Diagonal stop, you’ll first see Casa Batlo (on the right) and then La Pedrera (on your left) These ar all museums. If you want to visit, you’ll need to get a ticket. I’ve never been inside so I don’t know if is worth it or not. The street also has many designer shops on it. When you’ll keep walking, you’ll get to Plaza Catalunya which is big transit hub (with underground stops, bus stops, airport shuttle and so on). When you’ll keep walking down the street, you’ll get to famous la Rambla which is dividing the Old Town. It is a border between Barri Gòtic (on the left) and Raval (on the right).

I advise against eating or drinking on la Rambla with the exception of Boqueria Market. The street is bit of a tourist trap and everything there is just overpriced. In the middle of La Rambla is the Boqueria market – definitely worth visiting. They have everything there, from fruits, meats, fresh seafood to prepared little snacks, smoothies, puffy pastries and it’s just amazing. When you’ll keep walking down the street you’ll get to the seaside and a marina. Also, watch out for the pickpockets.

Parqc de Cuitadela and Arc de Triumf

Arc de Triomf

Parc de la Ciutadella s a park on the northeastern edge of Ciutat Vella. The rounds include the city zoo the Palau del Parlament de Catalunya, a small lake, museums, and a large fountain designed by Josep Fontserè (with possible contributions by the young Antoni Gaudí).
The Arc de Triomf was built by architect Josep Vilaseca i Casanovas as the main access gate for the 1888 Barcelona World Fair. The arch rosses over the wide central promenade of the Passeig de Lluís Companys, leading to the Ciutadella Park. It is just a stunning park and it is just an arch, but they are both pretty and photo-worthy. It's one of these things that pop up when you search for things in Barcelona.

City District: Ciutat Vella

Camp Nou

Camp Nou

Camp Nou s the home stadium of FC Barcelona since its completion in 1957. With a seating capacity of 99,354, it is the largest stadium in Spain and Europe, and the third largest football stadium in the world in capacity.

I think the most fun thing to do would be going for a FC Barcelona match (the Primera division matches aren't that expensive), but in June the season will probably be over.

You can still visit and walk around the stadium if you'll get one of the Barça Stadium Tour & Museum tickets.

Address: C. d'Arístides Maillol, 12, 08028 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Les Corts
