Sagrada Familia

Must See

Address: Carrer de Mallorca, 401, 08013 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Gràcia

Tickets available here


Parc Guell

Must See

Parc Guell is a park designed by Gaudi and is quite unexpected. It is one of these things that you just have to see when you’re in Barcelona. Park is bit higher in the hilly part it takes a little longer to get there from the nearest underground stop. The park itself has no admission and is open to public, but there’s a part with the colorful mosaics that you’ll need to get a ticket for. You can even get it on the same day as when you’ll be going. But it is only certain number of people permitted there at time, so you’ll be given a time slot.

Address: 08024 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Gràcia (north)

Tickets available here


Barcelona Cathedral & The Old Town

Old Town

Barcelona Cathedral also known as the Cathedral of the Holy Cross and Saint Eulalia, is a famous example of fifteenth-century Catalan Gothic architecture. Definitely worth seeing, as well as Barri Gotic which is the part of the town where the original cathedral is.

Address: Pla de la Seu, s/n, 08002 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Barri Gòtic


Plaza Espanya & Magic Fountain

Plaza Espanya

Magic Fountain is a “dancing” fountain on Plaza Espana. The shows are on between the Wed – Friday from 21:30 to 22:30. The show means that the fountain is coordinated with the light and music. Then on Plaza Espanya you also have National Art museum of Catalunya and Arenas. Arenas are old bullfighting ring. When bull fights were forbidden in Catalunya this one was converted in the shopping mall. Here you can go on the roof and there’s a nice view from there.

Address: 08004 Barcelona, Spain

City District: Sants-Montjuïc


Passeig de Gràcia & La Rambla

you mentioned that you’ll be staying in L'Eixample so you’ll be probably staying close to Passage de Gracia. It is just a long street on which you can find a few Gaudi’s buildings (which are definitely photo worthy).

When you’ll be walking down the street, you’ll first see Casa Batlo and then La Pedrera These ar all museums so if you want to visit, you’ll need to get a ticket. I’ve never been inside so I don’t know if is worth it or not. The street also has many designer shops on it. When you’ll keep walking, you’ll get to Plaza Catalunya which is big transit hub (with underground stops, bus stops, airport shuttle and so on). When you’ll keep walking down the street, you’ll get to famous la Rambla which is dividing the Old Town (it is a border between Barri Gòtic and Raval).

I advise against eating or drinking at la Rambla. It’s a tourist trap and everything there is just overpriced. In the middle of La Rambla is the Boqueria market – definitely worth visiting. They have everything there, from fruits, vegies, meats, fresh seafood to prepared little snacks, and smoothies, and puffy pastries and it’s just amazing. When you’ll keep walking down the street you’ll get to the seaside and a marina.

The Views

Tibidabo

is the mountain above Barcelona with beautiful overview of the city. At the top there’s a temple of the Sacred Heart of Jesus which looks like a Disney Castle. There’s also amusement park. To get there you’ll have to take I think underground and funicular but the view from up there is pretty amazing. Also, there’s amusement park there.


Montjuic Castle

you can take either bus or cable car to get there


Bunkers

these are the old bunkers above the city. I usually just took a bottle of wine or cava up there and watched sunset. The only downside is that it is bit of a hike from the metro (about 30 minutes up steep uphill) and it may be bit confusing to get there…but the view is worth it.

Food & Drinks

Boqueria Market

it is one of my favorite food places in the city just because it has bit of everything, and it is amazing. Just a small piece of advice when you enter don’t shop from the first stalls, these are for some reason pricier and you can probably find what you wanted somewhere, further back.


Blai 9

on Blai street in Poble Sec. This little street is full of pinchos and tapas places. Blai 9 is particularly nice because it is in a form of buffet, and you just pay for what you eat, and all the food is also beautifully styled.


Champaneria

yit’s a cava bar in el Born. Cava is basically Catalan champagne, they have different drinks too, but I recommend trying local cava when you’ll be there. You can also order appetizers like local olives, or cheeses … Everything you order can also buy in the deli section at the back. All is very good and surprisingly inexpensive


Chatelet

just a nice bar in Gracia which may be close to where you’ll be staying


Taller de Tapas

just a nice tapas place, there are multiple locations around the city and one is in Eixample
